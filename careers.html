Cabinet Maker

Halifax, NS Full-Time, Permanent
Apply Now

Position Overview

We are seeking a highly skilled and experienced Cabinet Maker to join our team. The ideal candidate will have a passion for woodworking, demonstrated expertise in custom cabinetry, and the ability to work independently or as part of a crew.

Key Responsibilities

  • Read and interpret technical drawings, blueprints, and shop drawings
  • Measure, cut, and assemble custom cabinets and millwork with accuracy
  • Perform laminating, edge banding, sanding, staining, and finishing
  • Fit, install, and adjust cabinetry on-site according to project plans
  • Operate woodworking machinery and power tools safely and efficiently
  • Maintain a clean, organized, and safe work environment
  • Collaborate with shop staff, project managers, and installers to ensure timely project completion

Requirements

  • Minimum 5 years of cabinet making experience, preferably in custom or architectural millwork
  • Proficiency with woodworking tools, CNC machines, and finishing equipment
  • Strong attention to detail and high-quality craftsmanship
  • Ability to read shop drawings and translate them into completed products
  • Comfortable working with a variety of materials including solid wood, veneer, MDF, and laminate
  • Ability to work independently and problem-solve on the go
  • Must provide 3 relevant cabinet shop references

Site Supervisor — General Contracting

Halifax Regional Municipality (HRM), NS Full-Time, Permanent
Apply Now

Position Summary

We are seeking an experienced and proactive Site Supervisor to oversee construction sites from start to finish. The ideal candidate has a background in commercial construction, excellent communication skills, and the ability to manage both in-house crews and subtrades to ensure safety, quality, and timelines are met.

Key Responsibilities

  • Supervise all on-site construction activities, ensuring adherence to project plans, schedules, and safety regulations
  • Coordinate and schedule trades, deliveries, and inspections
  • Conduct daily site walkthroughs and maintain detailed site logs
  • Monitor quality control and ensure compliance with building codes and company standards
  • Liaise with project managers, clients, architects, and engineers as needed
  • Address site challenges and resolve issues proactively to avoid delays
  • Enforce health and safety protocols and maintain a clean, organized site

Qualifications

  • Minimum 5 years of site supervision experience in general contracting, preferably with commercial projects
  • Strong understanding of construction processes, building codes, and trade coordination
  • Excellent leadership, organizational, and problem-solving skills
  • Ability to read and interpret blueprints and construction documents
  • Effective verbal and written communication skills
  • Must be able to work flexible hours, including occasional travel within HRM

Project Coordinator — Cabinetry & Millwork

Halifax, NS Full-Time, Permanent
Apply Now

Position Summary

We are seeking a highly organized and proactive Project Coordinator with experience in cabinetry and millwork to support our growing team. This role is responsible for managing multiple projects from scheduling through to delivery, and requires strong communication and planning skills.

Key Responsibilities

  • Oversee project scheduling and coordination from start to finish
  • Coordinate installations with general contractors and clients to ensure smooth execution
  • Review outgoing quotes for accuracy and completeness
  • Review awarded projects and compile detailed material lists
  • Order project-specific materials and follow up on delivery timelines
  • Manage supplier relationships and ensure timely material deliveries
  • Communicate with clients regarding project updates, changes, and expectations
  • Prepare and maintain comprehensive project documentation, including budgets, timelines, and reports
  • Use software tools such as Microsoft Excel and Word for project tracking and data management
  • Assist in drafting and technical design (experience with AutoCAD or cabinetry software is an asset)

Qualifications

  • Minimum 5 years of experience in cabinetry, millwork, or a related construction industry
  • Solid understanding of cabinetry products, materials, and vendor sourcing
  • Proficiency in Microsoft Excel and Word; knowledge of AutoCAD or cabinetry design software is an asset
  • Exceptional organizational and multitasking abilities
  • Strong communication skills and the ability to work effectively with clients, suppliers, and internal teams

Position Summary

  • Solid understanding of construction methods, tools, and materials
  • Ability to work independently and as part of a crew
  • Strong problem-solving skills and attention to detail
  • Valid driver's licence and willingness to travel within Colchester County
  • Red Seal certification or apprenticeship in progress is considered an asset
